GENIUS BRACESTORM

In today's fast paced world where immediate results are in demand, correction of common orthodontic problems are sometimes done with restorative procedures, promisingpatients with "instant smile" and "instant orthodontics." However, these "instant smiles" may oftentimes require unnecessary reduction of healthy tooth structure.

On the other hand, there are some problems with tooth malpositions, crowding and spaces that cannot be corrected with Orthodontics alone and may result in "overzealous orthodontics" to achieve the desired result.

Careful treatment planning can help the clinician in drawing the line between the need for restorative work and orthodontic treatment or the need for both.

A synergy between these two disciplines in Dentistry - Restorative and Orthodontics - does exist and can result in a most conservative approach in achieving a balanced occlusion and a beautiful smile.
